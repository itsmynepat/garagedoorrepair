Understanding Garage Door Springs

Garage doors typically utilize two types of springs: torsion springs and extension springs.

  • Torsion Springs: These are mounted above the garage door and are responsible for lifting the door by twisting and storing energy.
  • Extension Springs: Located on either side of the door, these springs stretch to help lift the door.

Both types of springs are under high tension and can be dangerous if not handled properly. Regular maintenance and timely replacement are crucial for ensuring safety and functionality.

Why You Might Need Spring Replacement

There are several reasons why you may need to replace your garage door springs:

  • Wear and Tear: Over time, springs can lose their tension due to regular use, leading to malfunction.
  • Temperature Changes: Fluctuations in temperature can affect the lifespan of your springs, causing them to break.
  • Improper Installation: If the springs were not installed correctly initially, they may fail prematurely.
  • Age: Most garage door springs have a lifespan of about 10,000 cycles; once they reach this limit, they should be replaced.

Signs You Need Garage Door Spring Replacement

Recognizing the signs that indicate your garage door springs need replacement can save you from further complications:

  • Loud Noises: If you hear a loud bang or snap when operating your garage door, it's likely a spring has broken.
  • Door Won't Open or Close: A broken spring will prevent your garage door from functioning correctly.
  • Uneven Door Movement: If your garage door moves unevenly or appears crooked when opening or closing, it may indicate a spring issue.
  • Visible Damage: Inspect your springs for any visible signs of wear, rust, or damage.
